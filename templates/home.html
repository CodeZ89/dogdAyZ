PetBook: A Centralized Rescue Database for Underserved Shelters Across the US

PetBook is a centralized database where a user may find animals that are available for adoption across the United States. In 2021, PetBook assisted in the rehoming of more than 2000 pets to approximately 1500 families, from hard to reach shelters across the country. PetBook also assists these shelters with finding eligible foster parents for animals that are unable to be housed at the shelter's home location. Currently PetBook works with over 100 shelters and 250 active foster homes across the US. PetBook uses a matchmaking algorithm to match pets to potential adopters, and adopters to potential pets. A database driven website that will record and facilitate the adoption of Pets that are available across the country in Shelters and Fosters (foster homes), and keep record of the families/people that may want to become Adopters.

Pets: An entity that records the pets that are currently in the database and available for adoption.

Adopters: An entity that contains information about potential and repeat adopters

Fosters: An entity that records the relevant information for current homes that pets are being fostered

Shelters: An entity that records the current shelters and their locations that are working with PetBook

Foster's Shelters: An intersection table that matches current fosters to the shelters that they are associated

Adoption Records: An entity that maintains the records of which pets have been adopted, the ID of the person that adopted them, and if the pet has been returned
